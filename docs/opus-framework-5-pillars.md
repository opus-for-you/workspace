# Opus Framework: 5 Pillars

The Opus Framework is a holistic system for managing professional development and client work. It's organized into five interconnected pillars that form a complete operating system for career ownership.

---

## ü™∑ 1. Purpose

*Why you do what you do ‚Äî the center of gravity*

### Subcomponents:

**Vision Statement**
- Long-term guiding intention for life and work

**Mission Statement**
- Focused description of what you do for others (clients, community)

**Core Values**
- Up to 5‚Äì7 principles that guide behavior and decisions
- Linked to evaluation criteria for projects or clients

**Ideal Impact**
- Describe how you want to affect others' lives through your work
- Aligns client goals with personal mission

**Motivation Archive**
- Collection of quotes, experiences, or reflections that remind you of your "why"
- Useful during burnout or decision paralysis

**Success Criteria (Subjective)**
- Non-metric-based feelings of success
- E.g., balance, creativity, peace, connection

---

## üï∞ 2. Rhythm

*Your relationship with time ‚Äî flow, cycles, and structure*

### Subcomponents:

**Annual Vision & Themes**
- Set a word or intention for the year; identify key priorities

**Quarterly Priorities**
- Goals broken into manageable, focused sprints

**Monthly Intentions & Reviews**
- Planning + reflection journal for work/life cadence

**Weekly Flow Template**
- Weekly planner with space for:
  - Focus areas
  - Big 3 tasks
  - Key meetings
  - Time-blocks or day themes

**Daily Practice Template**
- Morning check-in + evening review
- Mood, energy, key wins, priorities

**Time Budget**
- Visual or tabular map of where your hours go across client work, admin, learning, etc.

**Project Timeline Dashboard**
- Gantt view or timeline by client/project

**Seasonality & Energy Mapping**
- Personal productivity trends (e.g., time of day, time of year)

---

## üßë‚Äçü§ù‚Äçüßë 3. Network

*People and relationships ‚Äî clients, collaborators, community*

### Subcomponents:

**Client Directory**
- Master database: Contact, Services, Projects, Personality Notes, Session History

**Prospect Pipeline**
- CRM for leads: Status, Source, Follow-up date, Notes, Lead Score

**Collaboration Ledger**
- Current and past collaborators; shared work; agreements

**Stakeholder Map**
- Anyone influencing your work or decisions (mentors, clients' team members, etc.)

**Touchpoint Tracker**
- Track last interaction, next follow-up, status of relationship health

**Referrals & Testimonials**
- Track who has referred clients; store testimonials by client

---

## üèó 4. Structure

*The scaffolding of your work ‚Äî what you deliver and manage*

### Subcomponents:

**Project Dashboard**
- Kanban view for all active projects
- Status, Client, Deliverables, Due Dates, Linked Docs

**Task Management System**
- Linked to projects; supports recurring tasks and dependencies
- "Today", "This Week", "Later" views

**Service Catalog**
- Menu of offerings (with scope, price, outcomes)
- Great for productized service providers

**Resource Library**
- Templates, swipe files, PDFs, decks‚Äîorganized by purpose

**Client Portals (per client)**
- Shared view or space for key deliverables, comms, check-ins
- Optional integration or database filtered by client

**Outcome Tracker**
- Track ROI or impact of services
- Before/after metrics, feedback loops

---

## üõ† 5. Method

*How you work ‚Äî systems, tools, SOPs, and rituals*

### Subcomponents:

**SOP Library**
- Templates for how you do repetitive tasks
- Client onboarding, email setup, reporting

**Automations Map**
- Visual map or table of automations (e.g., Zapier, Make, Notion formulas)

**Client Journey Maps**
- Stages from first contact to offboarding
- Includes emotional journey (trust, clarity, results)

**Rituals & Routines**
- System for your own habits
- Weekly planning, inbox zero, energy reset

**Tools Stack Inventory**
- List of apps used and what they're used for
- Includes cost, links, and renewal dates

**Decision Frameworks**
- Criteria-based tools for choosing new clients, prioritizing work, etc.

---

## Application in Opus

The Opus app implements these pillars through:

1. **Purpose** ‚Üí North star vision, onboarding reflections
2. **Rhythm** ‚Üí 5-week program structure, weekly reviews
3. **Network** ‚Üí Connections management (unlocked week 2)
4. **Structure** ‚Üí Goals, tasks, project tracking
5. **Method** ‚Üí AI-assisted workflows, smart scheduling

Each week of the program focuses on different aspects of these pillars to build a complete professional operating system.
